# Sweeps and solvers

Two more powers the engine brings: a one-axis **sweep** for sensitivity, and
**solvers** that invert a dimensioned relation. Both keep units honest.

/// md
## Sweep — hydrostatic pressure vs depth

`sweep(start, end, n)` makes an axis; arithmetic broadcasts over it.

/// adder
from numbat import Q, sweep

depth    = sweep(Q(0, "m"), Q(100, "m"), 11)
rho      = Q(1000, "kg/m^3")
g        = Q(9.81, "m/s^2")
pressure = (rho * g * depth).to("kPa")
pressure

/// md
A swept quantity renders as `min … max unit` — here, 0 at the surface up to
~981 kPa at 100 m.

/// md
## solve_for — invert a relation

The callback takes and returns `Quantity` values; the bounds give the search
interval (and pin the answer's dimension). What radius gives a sphere of volume
1 m³?

/// adder
from numbat import solve_for
from math import pi

sphere_volume = lambda r: Q(4/3 * pi, "") * r ** 3
radius = solve_for(sphere_volume, Q(1, "m^3"), Q(0, "m"), Q(2, "m"))
radius

/// md
## maximize — projectile range

Range of a projectile launched at speed `v`, angle `theta`:
`R = v² · sin(2θ) / g`. Maximize over the launch angle.

/// adder
from numbat import maximize
from math import sin

v = Q(30, "m/s")
g = Q(9.81, "m/s^2")
# theta.magnitude is the canonical value of an angle — already radians
launch_range = lambda theta: (v ** 2 * sin(2 * theta.magnitude) / g).to("m")
best_angle = maximize(launch_range, Q(0, "deg"), Q(90, "deg"))
best_angle

/// md
The optimum lands at 45° (`best_angle ≈ 45 deg`), as it should for level
ground — recovered here numerically, with the range computed in real metres at
every step.
